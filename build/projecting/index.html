Capturing Performance

Understanding what it means to capture live art and performance whilst trying to encourage wellbeing and sustainability

In order to capture anything worth watching at a performance, you need a menagerie of microphones, cameras and a professional crew yet we still find ourselves trying to capture these events. Why?

They look awful and the sound quality follows suit. Sometimes we'll share these videos but it isn't about sharing a pleasant experience, so why do we share it?

Making yourself known

People have held up lighters and phones up at events for a lot longer than camera phones have existed. A big part of the experience is making ourselves known. It's a collective recognition of joy but was not traditionally used to go any further than the relationship between the people in the room. It's almost like we're attempting to bring others into this rich collective experience.

We also make ourselves known on digital platforms despite having nothing to say in particular. This can take the form of thoughtless text or custom emojis in the case of Twitch on the left below or spamming hearts on Periscope on the right.

Recording as performance

There is a performative aspect to recording. It adds to our self image and reinforces ideas that we have about ourselves. We project an ideal self through sharing these experiences. We are extrinsically motivated.

Dancing holds tension between being individually performative and collectively joyous. Being in an audience thrives on that experience. It helps us experience joy collectively, around the same art, while physically being in the same place and performing through dance.

It's also annoying actors and theater goers.

Policing and Protest

People also use social media in an attempt to emancipate and build movements. The Arab Spring used social media to organise and evade disruptive forces.

While sometimes, people use social media to police each other. At Coachella, people came together to find stolen phones using social media. They caught the person without having to rely on the police.
